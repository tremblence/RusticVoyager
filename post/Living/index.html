    Live  Well   ☯   Spend Less



There are three primary costs to traveling.

Food, Transportation and Lodging


Food can be cheap and delicious if bought from street markets & vendors. Transportation can be cheap if you take the bus or hitchhike. Lodging quickly adds up if you don't have a place to stay for free. Depending on which country you are in, hostels can be as low as $4/night. Most are around $10. You should have no trouble finding a hostel, but if there are none, hotels can be expensive.

▁   ▂   ▃   ▄   ▅    ▇    ▅   ▄   ▃   ▂   ▁        


A hostel is great option for $10/night. Many times you get access to showers, laundry, kitchen and maybe free breakfast. You get to chill with other travlers/locals and your money is likely feeding the local family.

In a way, thats an awesome deal for $10. But it can add up quick. Hostels often rely on volunteers and if you like the hostel and location, you may consider volunteering there in exchange for food and stay. I've done this many times and it can be an awesome experience.

You save money and the work can be fun. One time I had a deal worked out where I pocketed all the cash from breakfast, and my boss got all the money for dinner and the bar. Eventually you may feel the work isn't worth the rewards and then you know its time to move on.

▁   ▂   ▃   ▄   ▅    ▇    ▅   ▄   ▃   ▂   ▁        


Another great option is Couch Surfing. There's a website and app for it. There are profiles and you can leave "reviews" to get a better sense of how legitimate people are. I Couchsurfed once in Manhatten, we went out and ate his local Punjabi food and we ended up friends.

Of course be smart, if you get bad vibes play it safe. Overall I like it, a community of mostly great people offering to show you around their city and a couch/bed for a night or two. Since they are hooking you up, you should buy them a drink or something nice.

▁   ▂   ▃   ▄   ▅    ▇    ▅   ▄   ▃   ▂   ▁        



If you are feeling really adventurous you can camp. I only recommend this for remote areas like the mountains or the beach. I've had excellent times camping in Costa Rica for nights on end, with huge campfires and over a few days we had a trusted community of travel homies. In many places this isn't a viable option, buts it can be amazing.
