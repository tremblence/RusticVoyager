∡ Buying your Flight ∡



Booking Holdings owns Booking.com, priceline.com, KAYAK, agoda.com, rentalcars.com, and OpenTable. Expedia owns Expedia.com, Hotels.com, Hotwire.com, trivago, Venere.com, Travelocity, Orbitz, and HomeAway.

These mega-companies make over 95% of their money with by commission. Meaning they middle man the ticket to the buyer and get some money from the transaction.

You could give away $100 to a company simply because you didn't check other ticketing options. People often compare Orbitz with Expedia and they are both owned by the same company!

So what do I recommend? SkyScanner and Momondo often have the cheapest tickets. I also check "Secret Flying" and and certain airlines like Spirit and Interjet for Latin American trips.

If you are flexible, set your arrival location to "Everywhere" and date to "Cheapest Month" to see what pops up. I did this and found flight to Colombia for $86 out of Florida. I had friends in Florida so I combined the trips and it was very cheap overall.

If you know where/when you want to go, try and book no more than 2 weeks in advance. Often the best price is around a month in advance. Either way, you may save $10-40 which is not worth fretting over.
